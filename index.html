ABOUT
I trained in data analysis, visualization, and computational modeling as a scientist in the MacLean Laboratory of Computational Neuroscience. Patterns of cascading activity are my specialty.
During my doctoral work, I developed a framework for quantifying emergent patterns of coordination in the microscopic neural communication networks of neocortex. This paradigm offers interesting new directions for studying large-scale communication patterns in our online society. I am currently relishing a postdoctoral gap year, woodshedding python and broadening my technical background with new skills, like javascript and web scraping. I am on the job market for Fall 2018, searching for ethically oriented work in analyzing or communicating complex data.
