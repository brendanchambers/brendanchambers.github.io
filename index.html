ABOUT
Python. Temporal patterns in networks. Visualization. Writing.
I practiced data research and received my PhD in the MacLean Laboratory of Computational Neuroscience at the University of Chicago.
Before, I studied computer science at Oberlin College. My 2011 honors thesis was on deep networks, steered with attention.
Here are a few things I have worked on: Mapping communication in networks, cascading activity, signal acquisition, visualization, computational modeling of spiking neurons, statistics for comparing populations, non-differentiable optimization, recurrent networks
  • Chambers & MacLean 2015
  • Chambers & MacLean 2016
  • Chambers et al 2017
During my doctoral work, I developed a framework for quantifying emergent patterns of coordination in the microscopic neural communication networks of neocortex.

This paradigm offers new directions for studying large-scale communication patterns in our online society.
The end of my postdoctoral gap year is just a few months away now. I spent the year leveling up my scientific programming (so much python) and broadening my technology skills (javascript, web scraping, D3).
I also found time for some side projects like rock climbing, liberal politics, and the complete collection of Bach 2-part Inventions.
Coming to a job market near you Fall 2018, searching for ethically oriented work in analyzing & communicating data.
