biographical info
Python. Temporal patterns in networks. Visualization. Writing.
I practiced data research and received my PhD in the MacLean Laboratory of Computational Neuroscience at the University of Chicago.
Before, I studied computer science at Oberlin College. My 2011 honors thesis was on deep networks, steered with attention.
Here are a few things I have worked on:
communication cascades
recurrent neural networks
signal acquisition
scientific visualization
computational modeling of spiking neurons
statistics for comparing populations
non-differentiable optimization
Emergent temporal patterns in a complex communication network. Chambers & MacLean 2016.
My work suggests new directions for studying large-scale communication patterns in our online society.
