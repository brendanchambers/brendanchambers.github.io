ABOUT
I have worked in... signal acquisition, data analysis, visualization, computational modeling, statistics, mapping communication networks... as a scientist in the MacLean Laboratory of Computational Neuroscience. Patterns of cascading activity are my specialty.
During my doctoral work, I developed a framework for quantifying emergent patterns of coordination in the microscopic neural communication networks of neocortex. This paradigm offers interesting new directions for studying large-scale communication patterns in our online society.
The end of my postdoctoral gap year is just a few months away now. I spent the year leveling up my scientific programming (so much python) and broadening my technology skills (javascript, web scraping, D3). I also found time for some side projects (links todo).
